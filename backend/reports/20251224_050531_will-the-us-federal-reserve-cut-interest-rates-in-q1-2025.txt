## 3. Literature Review (Synthesis of Think Tank & Walled Garden Data)  
The literature review synthesizes 12 relevant reports from 13 analyzed think tank publications (average relevance: 0.283), revealing two dominant thematic schools despite the scenario's temporal invalidity.  

**Inflation Dynamics as Primary Constraint**  
A consensus school emphasizes energy and trade-related inflation as the most proximate determinants of monetary policy. Brookings Institution [2] demonstrates that global central banks remain constrained by imported inflation, particularly when energy prices surge due to supply shocks. This aligns with World Gold Council [4] findings that energy volatility drives reserve diversification, indirectly pressuring the dollar. RAND Corporation [5] and Brookings [6] provide granular evidence: OPEC+ production cuts and Gulf geopolitical instability directly amplify oil price volatility, feeding into core inflation metrics. Concurrently, Council on Foreign Relations [8] establishes that US-China trade tensions elevate input costs through supply chain reconfiguration ("friend-shoring"), creating persistent inflationary pressures that could delay monetary easing. This school posits rate cuts as contingent on sustained disinflation, which hinges on energy market stabilization and reduced trade friction.  

**Dollar Resilience and Structural Constraints**  
A second cluster examines the interplay between dollar strength and reserve diversification. Peterson Institute [1] argues that dollar strength—fueled by the Fed's relatively hawkish stance—creates a structural constraint on policy flexibility. The World Gold Council [4] corroborates this, noting emerging market central banks' strategic gold accumulation to diversify reserves away from dollar assets, which may indirectly influence Fed credibility. However, both schools treat the Fed as a passive reactor to external shocks rather than an active shaper of global financial conditions, revealing a critical gap in modeling internal decision-making processes.  

The walled garden analysis attempted NLP sentiment scoring of FOMC minutes for "dovish shift" markers but yielded no actionable results [Walled Garden Results, 2025], further confirming the absence of empirical data for the specified timeframe.
