## 6. Discussion (Implications, Policy Impact, Risks)  
### Implications  
The analysis demonstrates a critical methodological vulnerability: risk assessment frameworks can generate probabilistic outputs for non-events when primary evidence is absent. The 0.25 political risk score and 48% upside probability derive from ambient market signals rather than incident-specific data, highlighting the danger of algorithmic overextrapolation. This case exemplifies "narrative void filling," where analytical systems generate outputs from tangential data when core evidence is missing [Weak Signals, 2025].  

### Policy Impact  
The uniform "neutral/low" impact assessment across all policy domains indicates no actionable implications for aviation safety, bilateral relations, or disaster response. The market-focused risk score (0.25) suggests minimal portfolio volatility but provides no guidance for aviation regulators or diplomatic channels. Policy recommendations must therefore target analytical protocols rather than incident management:  
- Implement mandatory evidentiary thresholds before scenario activation  
- Introduce "null scenario" reporting protocols to prevent misattribution of market signals  
- Require source validation for all mention-based metrics  

### Risks  
Three critical risks emerge:  
1. **Analytical Misattribution**: Market volatility (e.g., oil price fluctuations) being erroneously linked to non-events, potentially triggering inappropriate policy responses [Think Tank Insights, 2025]  
2. **Resource Misallocation**: Diverting investigative capacity toward phantom incidents while genuine crises remain undetected  
3. **Erosion of Analytical Credibility**: Repeated generation of evidence-deficient assessments undermines stakeholder trust in forecasting systems [Weak Signals, 2025]  

The 35% confidence in the political risk score underscores the high uncertainty of market-derived assessments for non-events.
