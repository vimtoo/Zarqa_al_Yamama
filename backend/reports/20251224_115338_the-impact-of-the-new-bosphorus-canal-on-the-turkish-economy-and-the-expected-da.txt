## 3. Literature Review (Synthesis of Think Tank & Walled Garden Data)  
The literature review synthesizes 13 high-quality think tank reports and walled garden data, revealing critical contextual insights and profound research gaps.  

### Macroeconomic and Financial Context  
The global economic environment presents substantial headwinds for large-scale infrastructure projects. The Peterson Institute [2] demonstrates how Federal Reserve tightening and dollar strength increase borrowing costs for emerging markets, directly threatening the canal's foreign-currency-denominated financing. Brookings [3] highlights restrictive monetary policies that constrain fiscal space, while the Council on Foreign Relations [1] identifies supply chain fragmentation as a cost multiplier for specialized equipment procurement. These factors collectively establish a high-risk backdrop where external financial pressures pose unquantified threats to the project's viability.  

### Energy Security and Geopolitical Dimensions  
RAND Corporation [5] and Brookings [6] establish that energy security is increasingly precarious due to oil market volatility and OPEC+ fluctuations. The canal's strategic location intersects with these dynamics, potentially offering route diversification while introducing new vulnerabilities to geopolitical shocks and cyber threats [4]. The Carnegie Endowment [7] further details unstable Gulf security architecture, directly impacting regional energy flows. However, no literature synthesizes these risks specifically for the canal project.  

### Regional Economic Diversification Contrasts  
The Arab Gulf States Institute [8] provides contrasting insights on economic diversification, noting Kuwait's gradual approach through sovereign wealth funds and institutional reforms. This highlights a critical disconnect with the canal's singular-project strategy, revealing a gap in understanding how the canal integrates with Turkey's broader economic transformation framework.
